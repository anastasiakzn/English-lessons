Grammar · First Conditional

Real situations in the future

We use the First Conditional for a real or likely situation in the future and its result.
If + Present Simple,  will + verb
▸ The parts can swap places: We will be late if the train is delayed.
▸ A comma is used only when the if-part comes first.
▸ After if we use the present, not "will". ✗ If it will rain… → ✓ If it rains…
